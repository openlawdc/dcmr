                                  DC REGULATIONS

PROHIBITION OF RETALIATORY ACTS AGAINST TENANTS (§ 14-307)

307.1. No action or proceeding to recover possession of a habitation may be
   brought against a tenant, nor shall an owner otherwise cause a tenant to quit
   a habitation involuntarily, in retaliation for any of the tenant’s actions
   listed in § 307.3.

   307.2. No demand for an increase in rent from the tenant, nor decrease in the
   services to which the tenant has been entitled, nor increase in the
   obligations of a tenant shall be made in retaliation against a tenant for any
   of the tenant’s actions listed in § 307.3.

   307.3. This section prohibits the taking of any of the actions set forth in
   this section in retaliation against the tenant for any of the following
   actions by a tenant:
   
   
   
   (a)	A good faith complaint or report concerning housing deficiencies made to
   the owner or a governmental authority, directly by the tenant or through a
   tenant organization;
   
   
   
   (b)	The good faith organization of a tenant organization or membership in a
   tenant organization;
   
   
   
   (c)	The good fait assertion of rights under this subtitle, including rights
   under §§ 301 and 302 of this chapter, or § 101 of chapter 1.
   
   
   
   	SOURCE: The Housing Regulations of the District of Columbia, 5G DCRR § 2910,
   Commissioners’ Order 55-1503 (August 11, 1955).
