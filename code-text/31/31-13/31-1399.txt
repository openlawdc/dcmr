                                  DC REGULATIONS

DEFINITIONS (§ 31-1399)

1399.1. The words and phrases in this chapter shall have the meaning as set
   forth below:
   
   
   
   Applicant &#8211; An individual, partnership or corporation seeking a
   taximeter business license from the Commission.
   
   
   
   Commission &#8211; The DC Taxicab Commission.
   
   
   
   Driver &#8211; A person licensed by the Commission to drive a licensed DC
   taxicab in the District of Columbia.
   
   
   
   Hack-up &#8211; To outfit a vehicle as a taxicab and obtain approval from the
   Commission for that vehicle to serve as a taxicab for the first time.
   
   
   
   Mailing address &#8211; The address designated by an applicant or licensee for
   the receipt of all notices and correspondence from the Commission. Unless
   otherwise approved in advance, the mailing address of a taximeter business
   licensee shall be the street address of the business.
   
   
   
   Owner &#8211; An individual, partnership, limited liability company or
   corporation licensed by the Commission to own and operate a taxicab or
   taxicabs.
   
   
   
   Rate of fare &#8211; The established fare which may be charged by a licensed
   taxicab, which fare has been promulgated by the Commission, and which fare may
   include, but is not limited to surcharges and waiting times.
   
   
   
   Seal &#8211; A device, approved by the Commission, which may be installed on a
   taximeter, wire, wiring mechanism, gear or other device, so that no
   adjustment, repair, alteration or replacement can be made without removing or
   mutilating the seal or seals.
   
   
   
   Taximeter &#8211; An instrument or device approved by the Commission by which
   the charge to a passenger for hire of a licensed taxicab is automatically
   calculated and on which such charge is plainly indicated.
   
   
   
   Taximeter business &#8211; Any business which engages, in whole or in part, in
   the manufacture, sale (whether of new or used equipment), installation,
   repair, adjustment, testing, sealing or calibrating of taximeters, for use
   upon any licensed vehicle in the District of Columbia including any business
   which engages in whole or in part in the installation of taxicab cruiser
   lights.
   
   
   
   Taximeter business owner &#8211; An individual, partnership or corporation
   licensed by the Commission to own and operate a taximeter business.
   
   
   
   Taximeter test (sometimes alternatively referred to as &#8220;test&#8221;)
   &#8211; Shall mean a method to determine compliance with distance and time
   tolerances, utilizing either a road test over a precisely measured road course
   or a simulated road test determining the distance traveled by use of a roller
   device, or by computation from rolling circumference and wheel-turn data, said
   test having been conducted in accordance with the National Institute of
   Standards and Technology Handbook No. 44.
   
   
   
   Wiring harness &#8211; Any wire or collection of wires, including all
   connections thereto, which is connected in any manner whatsoever to a
   taximeter or in any way affects the operation of a taximeter.
