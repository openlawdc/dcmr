                                  DC REGULATIONS

DEFINITIONS (§ 4-1099)

1099.1. When used in this chapter, the following terms and phrases shall have
   the meaning ascribed:
   
   
   
   Commercial space &#8211; any building, structure, or designated portion
   thereof which is occupied as, or designed or intended for, occupancy as an
   accommodation for business activity, and any vacant land which is offered for
   sale or lease for the construction or location thereon of any building,
   structure, or portion of the building or structure.
   
   
   
   Dwelling &#8211; any building, structure, or portion of the building or
   structure, including a house or apartment, which is occupied as, or designed
   or intended for occupancy as, a residence by one or more families or business
   entities, and any vacant land which is offered for sale or lease for the
   construction or location thereon of any such building, structure, or portion
   thereof.
   
   
   
   Familial status &#8211; has the same meaning found in § 102 of the Act (D.C.
   Code § 1-2502).
   
   
   
   Family &#8211; a single individual or two or more related or unrelated persons
   who share a dwelling.
   
   
   
   Owner &#8211; has the same meaning as found in § 102 of the Act (D.C.
   Official Code § 2-1401.02 (2001)).
   
   
   
   Owner’s family &#8211; the owner’s spouse, the owner’s children, and the
   parents of the owner and the owner’s spouse.
   
   
   
   Person &#8211; has the same meaning as found in § 102 of the Act (D.C.
   Official Code § 2-1401.02(21) (2001)).
   
   
   
   Prohibited basis &#8211; any one or a combination of the following: race,
   color, religion, national origin, sex, age, marital status, personal
   appearance, sexual orientation, family responsibilities, matriculation,
   political affiliation, physical handicap, source of income, and place of
   residence or business of any individual.
   
   
   
   Real Property &#8211; land and generally whatever is erected or affixed to the
   land, including condominiums and cooperatives.
   
   
   
   Restrictive covenant &#8211; any written statement in a deed or other document
   related to the transfer of an interest in real property purporting to limit
   transfer, sale, or rental on the basis of race, color, religion, national
   origin, sex, age, marital status, personal appearance, sexual orientation,
   family responsibilities, matriculation, political affiliation, physical
   handicap, source of income, and place of residence or business of any
   individual.
   
   
   
   Sale &#8211; a transaction in real property where there is a transfer of
   ownership of and title to real property from one person to another for
   consideration.
   
   
   
   Source of income &#8211; has the same meaning as found in § 102 of the Act
   (D.C. Official Code § 2-1401.02(29) (2001)).
   
   
   
   To rent &#8211; leasing, subleasing, to let, or otherwise to grant for a
   consideration the right to occupy premises not owned by the occupant.
   
   
   
   Transaction in real property &#8211; has the same meaning as found in § 102
   of the Act (D.C. Official Code § 2-1401.02(30) (2001)).
