ESTABLISHMENT OF CLASSIFICATION AND COMPENSATION POLICY (ยง 7-1800)

1800.1. Authority to establish a new classification and compensation system
   for staff appointed by the District of Columbia Retirement Board
   (&#8220;Retirement Board&#8221;) is set forth in Title IX, section 902(d) of
   the Service Improvement and Fiscal Year 2000 Budget Support Act of 1999, D.C.
   Law 13-38 (the &#8220;Retirement Board Amendment Act of 1999&#8243;), and the
   District of Columbia Retirement Reform Act, as amended (the &#8220;Reform
   Act&#8221;), effective November 17, 1979 (Pub. L. 96-122, 93 Stat. 866). D.C.
   Code ยง 1-711(e)

   1800.2. Authority to convert Retirement Board staff attorneys who meet the
   qualifications for entry into the Legal Service and the Senior Executive
   Attorney Service, to implement a separate competitive salary structure, and to
   establish a performance management system for attorneys in the Legal Service
   and the Senior Executive Attorney Service employed by the Retirement Board is
   set forth in the Legal Service Establishment Act (&#8220;Legal Service
   Act&#8221;) of 1999, (D.C. Code ยง 1-609.51 et. seq.).

   1800.3. The Retirement Board&#8217;s principal mission, as derived from the
   findings and purpose section of the Reform Act (D.C. Code&#8217; 1-701), is to
   manage the assets of the D.C. Police Officers&#8217; and Fire Fighters&#8217;
   Retirement Fund, and the D.C. Teachers&#8217; Retirement Fund, (collectively
   the &#8220;Funds&#8221;) on an actuarially sound basis in order to provide
   proper financing for the benefits to which the District&#8217;s retired police
   officers, fire fighters and teachers are entitled.

   1800.4. Pursuant to authority cited in sections 1800.1 and 1800.2 above, the
   Retirement Board hereby establishes policy and program regulations for a
   classification and compensation system for staff appointed by the Retirement
   Board, a separate competitive salary structure, and a performance management
   system for attorneys eligible for entry in the Legal Service and Senior
   Executive Attorney Service employed by the Retirement Board, as required by
   the Legal Service Act.

   1800.5. The Retirement Board reserves the right to amend any part of this
   classification and compensation policy and program regulation based upon,
   without limitation, changes in fiscal condition, changes in operating
   requirements, and changes in applicable law.
