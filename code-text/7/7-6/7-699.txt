DEFINITIONS (ยง 7-699)

699.1. When used in this chapter the following terms shall have the meanings
   ascribed:
   
   
   
   Act &#8211; the Youth Employment Act of 1979 (D.C. Law 3-46).
   
   
   
   Dependent &#8211; any persons who is a resident of the District and for whom
   the participant provides support.
   
   
   
   Director &#8211; the Director of the Department of Employment Services, or his
   or her designee, contracting officer, representative of contractual agents.
   
   
   
   On-Job Training (OTJ) &#8211; training in the private sector or public sector
   given to a participant, who has been hired first by the employer, and which
   occurs while the participant-employee is engaged in productive work which
   provides knowledge or skills essential to the full and adequate performance of
   the job.
   
   
   
   Participant Employee &#8211; a person who has met the eligibility requirements
   of ยง601 and who is hired as an employee by an employer pursuant to an
   agreement specified in ยง601.
   
   
   
   Participating Employers &#8211; both profitmaking and non-profitmaking
   persons, associations, partnerships, and corporations, as well as public
   agencies which enter into formal agreements with the Director to hire
   participant-employees under the Adults with Dependents Program.
   
   
   
   Resident &#8211; a person who has a permanent dwelling or home in the District
   of Columbia. A District resident shall also include those persons who are
   permanent resident aliens, or other aliens who have been permitted to accept
   permanent employment in the United States by the U.S. Immigration and
   Naturalization Service.
   
   
   
   Task Force &#8211; for the Adults with Dependents Program, the body of persons
   specified in ยง2(a)(4) of the Act and as established by the Mayor.
