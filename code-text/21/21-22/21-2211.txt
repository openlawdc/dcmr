COURT ACTION IN LIEU OF COMPLIANCE ORDER OR CEASE AND DESIST ORDER (ยง 21-2211)

2211.1. After a notice of violation has been issued and the time for
   compliance has expired, the Director may, in his or her discretion, institute
   a court action for injunctive relief, damages, civil penalties, or recovery of
   any corrective action costs incurred by the District government pursuant to
   ยง19 of the Act, in lieu of proceeding through the administrative process to
   issue a proposed compliance order or proposed cease and desist order.

   2211.2. In order to require a person to correct a situation which immediately
   threatens health or the environment or to restrain any person from engaging in
   any unauthorized activity that immediately endangers or causes damage to
   public health or the environment, the Director may, in his or her discretion,
   seek a temporary restraining order in Court in lieu of seeking an immediate
   compliance order or cease and desist order.
   
   
   
   	SOURCE: Final Rulemaking publishing at 43 DCR 6347, 6354 (November 29, 1996).
