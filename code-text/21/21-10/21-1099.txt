                                  DC REGULATIONS

DEFINITIONS (§ 21-1099)

When used in this Chapter, the following words and phrases shall have the
meanings ascribed:



100 percent (100%) recyclable &#8211; capable of being collected, separated, and
recovered from the solid waste stream through the District’s recycling
programs, and either used again or reused in the manufacture or assembly of
another package or product.



Act &#8211; means the Anacostia River Clean Up and Protection Act of 2009,
effective September 23, 2009 (D.C. Law 18-55; D.C. Official Code § 8-102 et
seq.).



Disposable carryout bag &#8211; a bag of any material, commonly plastic or kraft
paper, which is provided to a customer at the point of sale to carry purchases.



Post-consumer recycled content &#8211; any material that has completed its use
as a consumer item and that would otherwise have been disposed of as municipal
solid waste, but that has instead been reused or reconstituted as a product or
raw material.



Reusable carryout bag &#8211; a bag with handles that is specifically designed
and manufactured for multiple reuse and is made of cloth, fiber, other
machine-washable fabric, or durable plastic that is at least two and one-quarter
millimeters (2.25 mm) thick.



Source: Final Rulemaking published at 57 DCR 7208, 7215 (August 13, 2010); as
amended by Errata Notice published at 59 DCR 12366 (October 26, 2012).
