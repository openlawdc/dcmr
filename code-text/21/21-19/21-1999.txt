                                  DC REGULATIONS

DEFINITIONS (ยง 21-1999)

1999.1. When used in this chapter, the following words and phrases shall have
   the meanings ascribed:
   
   
   
   Accuracy &#8211; the measure of bias or consistent departure from the known
   true value and is determined by using standards and duplicates.
   
   
   
   Act &#8211; the District of Columbia Water Pollution Control Act of 1984.
   
   
   
   Ambient &#8211; those conditions which would be normally expected to prevail
   in the waters of the District, including any legally permitted point and
   nonpoint source discharges of pollutants; or, in reference to a particular
   source of pollution, means those conditions which would be expected to prevail
   without the source of pollution such as in a free flowing, nontidal stream
   those conditions upstream of the particular source of pollution.
   
   
   
   Director &#8211; the Director of the Department of Consumer and Regulatory
   Affairs or his or her representative.
   
   
   
   Duplicate &#8211; the repeated analysis of split portions of the same sample.
   
   
   
   Field measurement &#8211; those physical, chemical, radiological and
   biological properties of samples which are measured in the field at the site
   of sample collection.
   
   
   
   Laboratory measurement &#8211; those physical, chemical, radiological and
   biological properties of a sample which are determined in a laboratory.
   
   
   
   Monitoring &#8211; the preparation of equipment, the collection of samples,
   the field and laboratory measurements, the preservation and preparation of the
   sample and the reporting and storage of the data collected.
   
   
   
   Precision &#8211; the measurement of closeness with which repeated analysis of
   a parameter agree and is determined from duplicate samples.
   
   
   
   Replicate &#8211; those determinations on samples from the same source
   environment.
   
   
   
   Sample &#8211; a discrete quantity of water, sediment, biota or other matter
   which is representative of the source or place from which it was taken.
   
   
   
   Spike &#8211; the addition of a known amount of a substance to sample matrix
   containing a measured background concentration of the substance and is useful
   for determining precision and accuracy.
   
   
   
   Standard &#8211; a prepared sample matrix containing a known amount of a
   substance and is useful for calibration and accuracy determinations.
   
   
   
   	SOURCE: Final Rulemaking published at 42 DCR 654, 657 (February 3, 1995).
