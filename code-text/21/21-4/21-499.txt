DEFINITIONS (ยง 21-499)

499.1. When used in this chapter, the following words and phrases shall have
   the meanings ascribed:
   
   
   
   Comparable periods -
   
   
   
   (a)	No change in occupancy;
   
   
   
   (b)	The same or like seasons; and
   
   
   
   (c)	The same or like number of days and billing periods.
   
   
   
   General Manager &#8211; The chief administrative officer of the District of
   Columbia Water and Sewer Authority or his or her designee.
   
   
   
   Occupant &#8211; any individual, corporation, association or partnership who
   holds or possesses a premises in subordination to the title of the owner of
   the premises, with the consent of the owner.
   
   
   
   Owner &#8211; any individual, corporation, association or partnership listed
   as the legal title holder of record.
   
   
   
   Petitioner &#8211; an owner, occupant, owner&#8217;s agent, applicant for
   certification to test backflow preventers or a holder of a certificate to test
   backflow preventers.
   
   
   
   Practicable &#8211; For purposes of Section 430, a finding made by the General
   Manager that it is feasible to individually meter each apartment on the
   premises, or that there exists a tenant group or association that meets the
   Department&#8217;s requirements.
   
   
   
   Privately owned water meters &#8211; meters required to be installed, at no
   cost to the District, for the measurement of water supplied and used by
   commercial and industrial users or by premises with three (3) or more separate
   dwelling units.
   
   
   
   Residential tenants &#8211; a tenant as defined by the District of Columbia
   Water and Sewer Operations Amendment Act of 1990 (D.C. Law 8-136), D.C. Code
   ยง 43-1651(3) (1990 Repl. Vol.).
   
   
   
   WASA &#8211; the District of Columbia Water and Sewer Authority or its lawful
   agent, successor in interest or designee.
