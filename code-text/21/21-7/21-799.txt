                                  DC REGULATIONS

DEFINITIONS (§ 21-799)

799.1. When used in this chapter, the words and phrases shall have the
   meanings ascribed:
   
   
   
   Approved &#8211; compliance with published standards specifically applicable
   to the device, method, thing, procedure, or facility under consideration and
   which standards have been approved by the Director or the Director&#8217;s
   agent.
   
   
   
   Abandoned Vehicle &#8211; a motor vehicle or a trailer left on public or
   private property for an extended period of time in an inoperable or hazardous
   condition and having only scrap value.
   
   
   
   Ashes &#8211; the residue from the burning of wood, coal, coke, or other
   combustible materials.
   
   
   
   Baler &#8211; a machine used to compress and bind a quantity of solid waste or
   other material.
   
   
   
   Bulky Waste &#8211; the large items of solid waste such as appliances,
   furniture, large auto parts, trees and branches, stumps, flotage, and the
   like.
   
   
   
   Carry Container &#8211; a container used to transfer solid wastes from
   premises to a collection vehicle.
   
   
   
   Catch Basin &#8211; an enlarged and trapped inlet to a sewer designed to
   capture debris and heavy solids carried by storm or surface water.
   
   
   
   Clean Condition &#8211; free of litter, debris, and weeds. (D.C. Law 8-31)
   
   
   
   Collector &#8211; any person who is engaged in the collection or
   transportation of solid waste.
   
   
   
   Combustible Rubbish &#8211; miscellaneous burnable materials.
   
   
   
   Composting &#8211; a controlled microbial degradation of organic waste
   yielding a nuisance free product of potential value as a soil conditioner.
   
   
   
   Construction and Demolition Wastes &#8211; the waste building materials and
   rubble resulting from construction, remodeling, repair, and demolition
   operation on houses, commercial buildings, pavements, and other structures.
   
   
   
   Debt Reserves &#8211; the estimated cost of anticipated capital improvements
   and repairs to the District&#8217;s solid waste disposal system including, but
   not limited to, landfill replacement costs, incinerator repairs, and the
   construction of any waste-handling facilities. (37 DCR 4242)
   
   
   
   Debt Retirement &#8211; the sum of principal and interest estimated by the
   District to be paid in the current fiscal year for the purpose of reducing the
   long term debt related to the solid waste disposal system. (37 DCR 4242)
   
   
   
   Disposal Area &#8211; any site, location, tract of land, area, building,
   structure or premises used or intended to be used for partial or total solid
   waste disposal.
   
   
   
   Director &#8211; the Director of the Department of Public Works or the
   Director&#8217;s representative, agent, or designee.
   
   
   
   Domestic Refuse &#8211; all those types of refuse which normally originate in
   a residential household or apartment house.
   
   
   
   Dwelling Unit &#8211; one or more habitable rooms forming a single unit which
   is used or intended to be used for living, sleeping, and preparation and
   eating of meals.
   
   
   
   Enclosed Collection Vehicle &#8211; a vehicle that is specifically made or has
   been adapted for the collection of solid waste refuse, having a watertight
   body, either entirely enclosed or having a cover made of metal or other rigid
   material, with only the loading hopper exposed. (24 DCR 6800)
   
   
   
   Estimated Material Processing Costs &#8211; the costs associated with the
   preparation, handling, and disposal of the various types of waste at the
   waste-handling facilities. These include prior fiscal year operating costs,
   estimated debt retirement or reserves, and other expenses attributable to
   operating the waste-handling facilities. (37 DCR 4242)
   
   
   
   Food Waste (garbage) &#8211; animal and vegetable waste resulting from the
   storage, handling, preparation, cooking, or serving of foods.
   
   
   
   Food Waste (garbage) Grinder &#8211; a device for pulverizing food waste
   (garbage) into the sanitary sewerage system.
   
   
   
   Hazardous Wastes &#8211; those wastes that can cause serious injury or disease
   during the normal storage, collection, and disposal process, including but not
   limited to explosives, pathological and infectious wastes, radioactive
   materials, and dangerous chemicals.
   
   
   
   Household Hazardous Wastes &#8211; small quantities of hazardous wastes
   generated from homes and similar sources that are exempt from federal
   regulations, but exhibit dangerous characteristics such as ignitability,
   corrosivity, reactivity, or toxicity. (D.C. Law 7-38).
   
   
   
   I-95 Complex Fee &#8211; the cost per ton that the District government pays to
   dispose of a specific waste type at the I-95 Resource Recovery, Land
   Reclamation, and Recreational Complex in Fairfax County, Virginia. (37 DCR
   4242)
   
   
   
   Incinerator &#8211; any equipment, device or contrivance and all appurtenances
   of that equipment used for the destruction by burning of solid, semi-solid,
   liquid, or gaseous combustible wastes.
   
   
   
   Incinerator Residue &#8211; solid wastes which result from industrial
   processes and manufacturing operations, such as factories, processing plants,
   repair and cleaning establishments, refineries, and rendering plants. (18 DCR
   16)
   
   
   
   Industrial Waste &#8211; solid wastes which result from industrial processes
   and manufacturing operations such as factories, processing plants, repair and
   cleaning establishments, refineries and rendering plants.
   
   
   
   Junk &#8211; a collection of sorted salvageable materials.
   
   
   
   Market Agreement Rate &#8211; the projected cost of disposing each recyclable
   commodity based on an average derived from the prices stated in the District
   of Columbia&#8217;s disposal agreements for the previous twelve (12) months,
   and adjusted to reflect published industry forecasts of changes in price. (37
   DCR 4242)
   
   
   
   Non-Combustible Refuse &#8211; miscellaneous refuse materials that are
   unburnable at ordinary incinerator temperatures of at least thirteen hundred
   degrees Fahrenheit (1300° F.).
   
   
   
   Open Dump &#8211; an area on which there is an accumulation of solid waste
   from one or more sources without proper cover materials.
   
   
   
   Operating Costs &#8211; any cost related to the daily operation of the
   waste-handling facilities, including but not limited to, the following:
   
   
   
   (a)	Personal Services:
   
   
   
   (1)	Salaries;
   
   
   
   (2)	Additional gross pay; and
   
   
   
   (3)	Fringe benefits; and
   
   
   
   (b)	Non-Personal Services:
   
   
   
   (1)	Supplies and materials;
   
   
   
   (2)	Utilities, communication and building rentals;
   
   
   
   (3)	Other services and charges provided by external parties;
   
   
   
   (4)	Equipment purchase and rental; and
   
   
   
   (5)	Subsidies and transfers. (37 DCR 4243)
   
   
   
   Person &#8211; any individual, firm, partnership, company, corporation,
   trustee, association, or any other private or public entity.
   
   
   
   Premises &#8211; a building, together with any fences, walls, sheds, garages,
   or other accessory buildings appurtenant to that building, and the area of
   land surrounding the building and actually or by legal construction forming
   one enclosure in which the building is located.
   
   
   
   Projected Tonnage &#8211; the solid waste tonnage for the prior fiscal year,
   adjusted to reflect the estimated changes in tonnage for the current fiscal
   year as presented in the &#8220;Comprehensive Solid Waste Management
   Plan.&#8221; (37 DCR 4243)
   
   
   
   Putrescible Wastes &#8211; wastes that are capable of being decomposed by
   microorganisms with sufficient rapidity as to cause nuisances from odors,
   gases, and similar objectionable conditions. Kitchen wastes, offal, and dead
   animals are examples of putrescible components of solid waste.
   
   
   
   Recycling Surcharge &#8211; a fee imposed on each ton of solid waste disposed
   at the waste-handling facilities, computed by adding all costs associated with
   the handling and disposal of recyclable commodities. Computation of the
   surcharge includes, but is not limited to, the sum of (administrative costs
   attributable to the Office of Recycling, its activities and programs; costs
   attributable to municipal collection of recyclable commodities; market
   agreement rates; special handling costs; debt retirement; debt reserve)
   divided by projected tonnage. (37 DCR 4243)
   
   
   
   Refuse &#8211; see solid waste.
   
   
   
   Residue &#8211; the solid materials remaining after burning, comprising ash,
   metal, glass, ceramics, and unburned organic substances.
   
   
   
   Rubbish &#8211; nonputrescible solid wastes (including ashes) consisting of
   both combustible and non-combustible wastes, such as paper, cardboard, tin
   cans, yard rubbish, wood, glass, bedding, crockery, or litter of any kind.
   
   
   
   Solid Waste Disposer &#8211; a person who is a resident of the District of
   Columbia and who is not required by regulation to obtain a solid waste
   collector&#8217;s license in the District of Columbia (37 DCR 4243)
   
   
   
   Solid Waste Refuse &#8211; putrescible and nonputrescible solid wastes, except
   body wastes, and including abandoned vehicles, food waste (garbage), rubbish,
   ashes, incinerator residue, street cleanings, tree debris, and solid market
   and industrial wastes.
   
   
   
   Solid Waste Storage &#8211; the temporary on-site storage of solid waste.
   
   
   
   Special Handling Costs &#8211; the extraordinary costs associated with the
   handling of a specific waste type at the waste-handling facilities. (37 DCR
   4243)
   
   
   
   Street Refuse &#8211; material picked up by manual or mechanical sweeping of
   alleys, streets and sidewalks, litter from public litter receptacles, and dirt
   removed from catch basins.
   
   
   
   Supercans &#8211; a mobile refuse container on wheels having a serial number
   beginning with a DC prefix provided by the District to eligible premises
   specifically for use in the storage and collection of household refuse. (D.C.
   Law 5-20)
   
   
   
   Waste &#8211; useless, unwanted, or discarded materials resulting from normal
   community activities. The term &#8220;waste&#8221; includes solids, liquids,
   and gases. Solid wastes are classed as refuse. The term &#8220;waste&#8221;
   also include, but is not limited to, notices, handbills, leaflets, flyers and
   any other advertising or informational material. (18 DCR 16 &#038; D.C. Law
   7-38).
   
   
   
   Waste-Handling Facilities &#8211; the waste disposal facilities owned by,
   operated by, or under contract or agreement with the District of Columbia,
   including but not limited to, the Fort Totten Transfer Station, the Solid
   Waste Reduction Center No. 1, or the I-95 Resource Recovery, Land Reclamation,
   and Recreation Complex in Fairfax County, Virginia. (37 DCR 4243)
   
   
   
   Weeds &#8211; uncultivated or wild vegetation that is greater than four inches
   (4 in.) in height. (D.C.
   
   Law 8-31)
   
   
   
   Yard Rubbish &#8211; prunings, grass clippings, weeds, leaves, and general
   yard and garden wastes.

   799.2. Terms and phrases used in §§730 through 741 of this chapter shall
   have the meaning as ascribed in §2 of the Solid Waste Facility Permit Act of
   1995, D.C. Code §6-3451, unless expressly defined in this subsection:
   
   
   
   Act &#8211; Solid Waste Facility Permit Act of 1995, effective February 27,
   1996 (D.C. Law 11-94; D.C. Code §6-3451 et seq. (1995 Repl. Vol.)).
   
   
   
   Department &#8211; the Department of Consumer and Regulatory Affairs.
   
   
   
   Director &#8211; the Director of the Department of Consumer and Regulatory
   Affairs, or the Directors representative, agent, or designee.
   
   
   
   Disease Vector &#8211; Rodents, flies, mosquitoes, cockroaches, or other
   animals or insects capable of transmitting disease to humans.
   
   
   
   Operator &#8211; the person or persons possessing or exercising operational or
   managerial control over the solid waste facility.
