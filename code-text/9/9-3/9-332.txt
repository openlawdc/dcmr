TIME LIMITS FOR FILING FORMS (ยง 9-332)

332.1. The information required to be accurately completed on the income and
   expense form shall be mailed to the Office at the address provided in that
   form or in any accompanying instructions and must be postmarked not later than
   April 15th of the year in which the forms are mailed to affected taxpayers.

   332.2. The income-expense forms will be mailed to affected taxpayers at least
   thirty (30) days prior to the due date provided for in ยง331.1.

   332.3. When, in the opinion of the Deputy Chief Financial Officer, an income
   and expense form submitted prior to the deadline set forth in ยง 331.1 has not
   been accurately completed (that is, it is either inaccurate or incomplete, or
   both), the Deputy Chief Financial Officer shall inform the affected taxpayer
   (or the taxpayer&#8217;s agent) as soon as possible and request, in writing,
   that the form be accurately completed and delivered not later than thirty (30)
   days from the date of the mailing of the notice or as otherwise specified.

   332.4. Extensions of time, not to exceed thirty (30) days, to submit the forms
   may be granted at the discretion of the Deputy Chief Financial Officer for
   good cause.

   332.5. A request for an extension to file shall be delivered to the Deputy
   Chief Financial Officer not later than April 1st of the year in which the
   forms are to be mailed to affected taxpayers. Requests for extensions
   delivered after April 1st shall not be granted.
