DELEGABLE AND NON-DELEGABLE POWERS OF THE OFFICE OF ADJUDICATION (ยง 16-1528)

1528.1. Except as noted in ยง1528.2, all functions of the Office of
   Adjudication are to be performed personally by the Administrative Law Judge
   (ALJ). The following powers shall be nondelegable:
   
   
   
   (a)	Presiding over hearings;
   
   
   
   (b)	Admitting evidence;
   
   
   
   (c)	Ruling on motions; and
   
   
   
   (d)	Rendering final decisions in a case.

   1528.2. The staff of the Office of Adjudication may perform the following
   functions:
   
   
   
   (a)	Receive papers for filing;
   
   
   
   (b)	Issue summonses;
   
   
   
   (c)	Maintain the calendar for the Office of Adjudication;
   
   
   
   (d)	Perform internal administrative functions of the Office of Adjudication;
   
   
   
   (e)	Contact the parties when necessary concerning changes in schedules; and
   
   
   
   (f)	Exercise any other powers or duties delegated by the ALJ.
   
   
   
   	SOURCE: Final Rulemaking published at 33 DCR 6176, 6189 (October 10, 1986).
