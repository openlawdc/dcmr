                                  DC REGULATIONS

DEFINITIONS (ยง 16-599)

599.1. When used in this chapter, the following terms and phrases shall have
   the meanings ascribed:
   
   
   
   Advertise &#8211; the use of any method to bring a closing-out or fire sale to
   the attention of the public, including without limitation any advertising or
   notice appearing in any newspaper, magazine, or other publication, or in any
   handbill, poster, bill, circular, pamphlet, letter, sign, placard, card
   display, label, or telegram; or broadcast as a part of any radio or television
   program.
   
   
   
   Closing-out sale &#8211; as defined in D.C. Code ยง 47-2101, any sale in
   connection with which any representation by the person conducting the sale
   that the sale is being conducted, or is required or compelled to be conducted,
   for reasons of economic or business distress, inability to continue business
   at the same location, or the age or health of the owner or owners of the
   business. The term &#8220;closing out sale&#8221; shall include but not be
   limited to, all sales advertised, represented, or held forth under the
   designation of &#8220;going out of business,&#8221; &#8220;discontinuance of
   business,&#8221; &#8220;selling out,&#8221; &#8220;liquidation,&#8221;
   &#8220;lost our lease,&#8221; &#8220;must vacate,&#8221; &#8220;forced
   out,&#8221; &#8220;removal,&#8221; or any other designation of like meaning.
   
   
   
   Fire sale &#8211; a sale of goods, wares, or merchandise damaged by fire,
   smoke, water, or otherwise.
   
   
   
   Sale &#8211; when not qualified by &#8220;closing-out&#8221; or
   &#8220;fire,&#8221; means both &#8220;closing-out sale&#8221; and &#8220;fire
   sale.&#8221;
