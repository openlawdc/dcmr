DEFINITIONS (ยง 24-1399)

1399.1. When used in this chapter, the following terms and phrases shall have
   the meanings ascribed:
   
   
   
   Abatement Infractions &#8211; all residential, commercial and general
   violations for which abatement of the alleged nuisance is necessary or
   required before the case can be closed.
   
   
   
   Commercial Violations &#8211; describes those code infractions committed by or
   at any establishment that does not receive District solid waste collection
   service.
   
   
   
   Fine Amount &#8211; the monetary sanction assigned to the violation.
   
   
   
   General Violations &#8211; describes those code infractions committed by any
   person(s) within the boundaries of the District of Columbia who at the time of
   the violation is not then associated with any particular residence or
   commercial establishment.
   
   
   
   Legal Holiday &#8211; any holiday observed by the Government of the District
   of Columbia.
   
   
   
   Non-Abatement Infraction &#8211; all residential, commercial and general
   violations for which abatement of the alleged nuisance is not required.
   
   
   
   Public Emergency &#8211; for the purposes of this chapter a public emergency
   shall be defined as any situation where the health safety, property, or
   welfare of persons or property in the District of Columbia is or maybe
   threatened.
   
   
   
   Residential Violations &#8211; describes those code infractions committed by
   or at any establishment which receives District solid waste collection service
   (residential buildings containing three (3) or fewer dwelling units).
   
   
   
   Respondent &#8211; the party to whom the Notice of Violation is issued
   pursuant to the provisions of ยง4(c)(2) of the Litter Control Administration
   Act of 1985 (D.C. Law 6-100, D.C. Code ยง6-2903(c)(2) (1989 Repl.)), as
   amended. (37 DCR 6057)
   
   
   
   Service Hours &#8211; the number of hours of alternate community service
   required to satisfy the violation, if requested by the respondent.
   
   
   
   	SOURCE: Final Rulemaking published at 34 DCR 7807, 7828 (December 4, 1987);
   as amended by Final Rulemaking published at 37 DCR 6057 (September 14, 1990).
