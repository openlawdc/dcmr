DEFINITIONS (ยง 24-3799)

3799.1. When used in this chapter, the following terms and phrases shall have
   the meanings ascribed below:
   
   
   
   Act &#8211; the Urban Forest Preservation Act of 2002, effective June 12, 2003
   (D.C. Law 14-309; D.C. Official Code ยง 8-651.01 et seq.).
   
   
   
   ANSI(a)(300) standards &#8211; the American National Standards Institute, Inc.
   American National Standard Specifications for Standards for Tree Care
   Operations; Tree, Shrub, and Other Woody Plant Maintenance &#8211; Standard
   Practices
   
   
   
   Caliper &#8211; the diameter (width) of the trunk of a tree when measured at a
   height of six inches (6 in.) above the ground/soil.
   
   
   
   Circumference &#8211; the linear distance around the trunk of a tree when
   measured at a height of four and one-half feet (4 1/2 ft.) above the ground.
   
   
   
   District&#8217;s right-of-way &#8211; all the publicly owned property between
   the property line on a street, park, or other public property as such property
   lines are shown on the records of the District, and includes any roadway, tree
   space, sidewalk, or parking between such property lines.
   
   
   
   Hazardous Tree &#8211; a Special Tree that should be removed because it is:
   
   
   
   (a)	Structurally defective, diseased, dying, or dead;
   
   
   
   (b)	Posing a high risk of failure or fracture with the potential to cause
   injury to people or damage to property: or
   
   
   
   (c)	Causing damage to property or structures that cannot be mitigated in any
   manner other than removal of the tree.
   
   
   
   Permittee &#8211; a person or non-governmental entity issued a Special Tree
   Removal Permit by the Urban Forestry Administration.
   
   
   
   Person or non-governmental entity &#8211; any individual, corporation, firm,
   agency, association, organization, or utility company.
   
   
   
   Private Property &#8211; real property, including real property owned or under
   the jurisdiction of the District of Columbia and real property that is to be
   developed pursuant to an approved campus plan, planned unit development, or a
   historic preservation review. This term does not include the District&#8217;s
   right-of-way.
   
   
   
   Remove &#8211; cutting down, topping, girdling, breaking, or destroying a
   Special Tree.
   
   
   
   Replacement tree &#8211; A tree planted pursuant to ยง 3701.7(b).
   
   
   
   Special Tree &#8211; a tree within the District of Columbia that has a minimum
   circumference of fifty-five inches (55 in.).
   
   
   
   Tree Fund &#8211; the fund established under section 107 of the Urban Forest
   Preservation Act of 2002, effective June 12, 2003 (D.C. Law 14-309; D.C.
   Official Code ยง 8-651.07).
