                                  DC REGULATIONS

RULEMAKING HEARINGS (§ 3-5525)

5525.1. At its discretion, the Board may from time to time schedule public
   hearings to obtain comment on a proposed rulemaking. The rules of procedure
   set forth in this section shall apply to rulemaking hearings.

   5525.2. Any person may appear at a rulemaking hearing and may present, within
   the time limits determined by the Board, evidence, testimony, or argument that
   is relevant and not unduly repetitious.

   5525.3. No person shall have the standing of a party in a rulemaking hearing.

   5525.4. In those instances in which a petition for rulemaking has been filed
   with the Board by a member of the public pursuant to the District of Columbia
   Administrative Procedure Act, and where the Board in its discretion schedules
   a public hearing, the order of procedure at the hearing shall be as follows:
   
   
   
   (a)	Call to order and opening statement by the Chairperson and Board 	members;
   
   
   
   (b)	Introductory statement by the Director, General Counsel, or other staff
   	designated by the Board;
   
   
   
   (c)	Consideration of pending motions and procedural matters;
   
   
   
   (d)	Petitioner’s case;
   
   
   
   (e)	Persons in support of the petition; and
   
   
   
   (f)	Persons in opposition to the petition.
   
   
   
   
   
   SOURCE: Final Rulemaking published at 60 DCR 747 (January 25, 2013).
