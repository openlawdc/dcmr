                                  DC REGULATIONS

AFFIRMATION OF WRITE-IN CANDIDACY OF AN APPARENT WINNER OF A CONTEST BY WRITE-IN
VOTES IN A PRIMARY OR GENERAL ELECTION (§ 3-604)

604.1. Prior to certifying the ballots for a write-in candidate who is the
   apparent winner of a contest in a primary or general election, there shall be
   a determination as to that individual’s eligibility to be a candidate for
   nomination or election to the office for which the individual’s name was
   written or placed upon the ballot.

   604.2. If the Executive Director or his or her designee, determines that the
   apparent winner of a primary, general, or special election is a write-in
   candidate who has not previously filed a Declaration of Candidacy and
   Affidavit of Qualifications with the Board, that individual shall immediately
   be notified by hand delivery or by mail of the results of the election.

   604.3. In the case of a primary election, a write-in nominee, who is an
   apparent winner and wishes to perfect his or her candidacy shall file with the
   Board an Affirmation of Write-in Candidacy, on a form provided by the Board,
   not later than 4:45 p.m. on the third (3rd) day immediately following the
   election.

   604.4. In the case of a general election, an apparent winner by write-in
   voting, who wishes to perfect his or her candidacy shall file with the Board
   an Affirmation of Write-in Candidacy, on a form provided by the Board, not
   later than 4:45 p.m. on the seventh (7th) day immediately following the
   election.

   604.5. Write-in candidates for a nomination or election, who fail to submit
   the documents required by this section within the prescribed times, shall be
   deemed to be ineligible; and votes cast for such individuals shall be deemed
   invalid.

   604.6. The Affirmation of Write-in Candidacy form shall contain the same
   information required for the Declaration form set forth in § 601.3.

   604.7. Each write-in candidate shall swear or affirm upon oath before a
   District of Columbia notary or Board official that the information provided in
   the Affirmation of Write-in Candidacy is true to the best of his or her
   knowledge and belief.
