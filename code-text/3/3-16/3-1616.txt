                                  DC REGULATIONS

WRITE-IN CANDIDATES (ยง 3-1616)

1616.1. Write-in candidacies shall be permitted for any office appearing on
   primary, general or special election ballot.

   1616.2. To petition the Board for authorization to have pollwatchers and count
   observers on election day, a write-in candidate shall file with the Board, on
   or before 4:45 p.m. of the fourteenth (14th) day preceding the day of
   election, a Declaration of Candidacy and Affidavit of Qualifications on the
   form prescribed by the Board, and a petition authorizing pollwatchers and
   countwatchers pursuant to ยง 705 of this title.

   1616.3. To be eligible to receive the nomination of a political party for
   public office, a write-in candidate shall be a duly registered member of the
   party nominated and shall meet all other qualifications required for election
   to the office, and shall declare his or her candidacy no later than 4:45 p.m.
   on the third (3rd) day immediately following the date of the election on a
   form or forms prescribed by the Board.

   1616.4. To be eligible for election to public office, a write-in candidate
   shall be as follows:
   
   
   
   (a)	A duly registered elector;
   
   
   
   (b)	Meet all of the other qualifications required for election to the office;
   and
   
   
   
   (c)	Declare his or her candidacy no later than 4:45 p.m. on the seventh (7th)
   day immediately following the date of the election in which he or she was a
   candidate on a form or forms prescribed by the Board.
   
   
   
   	SOURCE: Final Rulemaking published at 36 D.C. Reg. 8364, 8371 (December 15,
   1989); and further amended by Final Rulemaking published at 43 D.C. Reg. 1078,
   1110 (March 1, 1996).
