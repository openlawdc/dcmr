                                  DC REGULATIONS

TICKETS (§ 19-2210)

2210.1. No person, except members of the Metropolitan Police Department
   assigned to duty at an event, may be admitted to the event without a ticket.

   2210.2. Each ticket, except those for the working press, officials, employees,
   and official photographers, shall have the price, name of the promoter, date,
   and place of the event printed plainly on it.

   2210.3. No promoter shall sell a ticket at a price other than the price which
   appears on the ticket.

   2210.4. No promoter shall change a ticket price or the place or date of an
   event without the approval of the Commission.

   2210.5. Tickets of different prices shall be printed on cardboard of different
   colors.

   2210.6. An inspector shall check the number and location of ticket boxes at
   the gates, ensure that the ticket boxes are sealed and padlocked, and open the
   ticket boxes and count the tickets after the event.

   2210.7. Each ticket issued to the press shall be marked “Press”.

   2210.8. A maximum of two percent (2%) of the tickets to an event may be
   complimentary.

   2210.9. Each complimentary ticket shall be marked “Complimentary”.

   2210.10. The promoter shall provide to the Commission, prior to the
   commencement of an event at which admission is charged, a manifest or report
   on the number, kind, and price of tickets printed for the contest.

   2210.11. Each ticket shall be separated from the stub when entering through a
   gate.  No person without a stub may occupy a seat.

   2210.12. After each event where an admission fee is charged, the promoter and
   the inspector shall submit and sign a detailed report on the results of the
   event, attendance, number of tickets sold at various prices, and total gate
   receipts.

   2210.13. At each event, representatives of the promoter and the inspector of
   the Commission shall supervise the gates and the gate receipts.

   2210.14. The inspector shall ensure that the box in which tickets are
   deposited has two (2) padlocks.  The Commission or its officials shall have
   the key or combination of one lock and the promoter shall have the key or
   combination of the other lock.
   
   
   
   	SOURCE: Final Rulemaking at 35 DCR 3753, 3756-57 (May 20, 1988).
