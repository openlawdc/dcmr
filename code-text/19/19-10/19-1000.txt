                                  DC REGULATIONS

GENERAL PROVISIONS (§ 19-1000)

1000.1. It is the policy of the District of Columbia to promote safety for
   persons and property in connection with the use and operation of vessels in
   District waters.

   1000.2. The District’s Harbor Master shall regulate the operation,
   navigation, mooring, and anchoring of all vessels and amphibian aircraft in
   the District waters, and shall enforce all laws and regulations relating to
   those waters.

   1000.3. The Mayor is authorized to conduct a boating safety and education
   program.

   1000.4. Any person who violates any provision of this chapter for which no
   specific penalty is otherwise provided, or who violates any provision of the
   regulations promulgated pursuant to this chapter, shall, upon conviction, be
   punished by a fine of not more than three hundred dollars ($300) or by
   imprisonment for not more than ten (10) days, or both.

   1000.5. Any person who violates any provision of this Article for which no
   specific penalty is otherwise provided or who violates any provision of the
   regulations promulgated pursuant to this Article shall, upon conviction, be
   punished by a fine of not more than $300.00 or by imprisonment for not more
   than ten days, or both.  The penalties imposed by this chapter shall be in
   addition to, and not in lieu of, any penalties imposed by any other law or
   regulation.
   
   
   
   	AUTHORITY: Unless otherwise noted, the authority for this chapter is an Act
   to amend section 895 of the Code of Law of the District of Columbia, 48 Stat.
   963 (July 15, 1934).
   
   
   
   	SOURCE: Article 29 §§ 1, 3, 22, 26 of the Police Regulations of the
   District of Columbia (January 1983).
