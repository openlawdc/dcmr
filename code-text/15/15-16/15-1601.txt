                                  DC REGULATIONS

NOTICE OF REMOVAL AND PETITION FOR TEMPORARY STAY (ยง 15-1601)

1601.1. A utility company shall provide a cable television system operator no
   less than sixty (60) days written notice prior to the following:
   
   
   
   (a)	Removal of facilities or termination of any service to those facilities,
   such removal or termination arising out of a rate, term or condition of a
   cable television pole attachment agreement; or
   
   
   
   (b)	Any increase in pole attachment rates.

   1601.2. A cable television system operator may file a &#8220;Petition for
   Temporary Stay&#8221; of the action contained in a notice received pursuant to
   ยง1601.1, within fifteen (15) days of receipt of the notice. This submission
   shall not be considered unless it includes a copy of the notice and states in
   concise terms, the relief sought, the reasons for the relief, including a
   showing of irreparable harm and likely cessation of cable television service.
   The named respondent shall file a answer within seven (7) days of the date the
   Petition for Temporary Stay was filed.

   1601.3. No further filings under ยง1601.2 shall be considered unless requested
   or authorized by the Commission and no extensions of time shall be granted
   unless justified pursuant to Commission rules.
   
   
   
   	SOURCE: Final Rulemaking published at 31 DCR 6496, 6497 (December 21, 1984).
