                                  DC REGULATIONS

AWARD WITHOUT DISCUSSIONS (ยง 27-1633)

1633.1. Discussions are not necessary:
   
   
   
   	 (a)	If prices are fixed by law or regulation; or
   
   
   
   (b)	It can be clearly demonstrated from the existence of full and open
   competition or prior cost experience with the goods or services that
   acceptance of the most advantageous initial proposal without discussion would
   result in a fair and reasonable price.

   1633.2. If discussions are not held pursuant to ยง 1633.1(b), the contracting
   officer may elect to award the contract to the highest ranked offeror,
   provided that:
   
   (a)	All offerors have been notified of the possibility that an award might be
   made without discussions; and
   
   
   
   	(b)	The terms of the contract are within the scope of the RFP; and
   
   
   
   (c)	The award must be made without any written or oral discussions with any
   offeror.
