                                  DC REGULATIONS

DONATION OF DISTRICT SURPLUS PROPERTY (ยง 27-906)

906.1. The District may donate its surplus personal property to an
   organization qualified 			as a tax-exempt under Section 501 of the Internal
   Revenue Code of 1986 (26 			U.S.C. ยง 501), or state, county, or municipal
   jurisdictions only after an attempt 			has been made to:
   
   
   
   
   
   
   
   Transfer the property within an agency;
   
   
   
   
   
   
   
   Transfer the property between agencies; and
   
   
   
   
   
   
   
   Auction the property for sale.

   906.2. The CPDO must determine whether an applicant is eligible to receive a
   donation 			of District surplus property, and re-verify its eligibility every
   two (2) years.

   906.3. Before receiving a donation of District surplus personal property, a
   tax-exempt 			organization must:
   
   
   
   
   
   
   
   Demonstrate it meets any approval, accreditation, or licensing requirements
   for operation of its program;
   
   
   
   
   
   
   
   Certify that it is not debarred, suspended, or excluded from any federal or
   District program, including procurement programs;
   
   
   
   
   
   
   
   Operate in compliance with applicable federal nondiscrimination law; and
   
   
   
   
   
   
   
   Certify that it is a non-profit tax-exempt organization.

   906.4. SPD shall maintain a donee wish-list which shall serve as a fundamental
   tool 			designed to neutralize the necessity of frequent visits to the
   warehouse in search 			of desired properties.  SPD employees shall be guided
   by these requests in the 			screening and selection of property.
   
   
   
   
   
   SOURCE: Final Rulemaking published at 60 DCR 9345 (June 21, 2013).
