                                  DC REGULATIONS

DISCHARGE AND AFTERCARE PLANNING (ยง 29-6275)

6275.1. For each resident, the facility shall prepare a discharge plan,
   including:
   
   
   
   (a)	A statement of the reason for the discharge;
   
   
   
   (b)	A summary of the services that were provided during care;
   
   
   
   (c)	A summary of the resident&#8217;s educational and medical histories;
   
   
   
   (d)	A description of the supports and resources to be provided to the resident
   in preparation for discharge;
   
   
   
   (e)	Recommendations for aftercare and the identity of the person(s)
   responsible for aftercare;
   
   
   
   (f)	The resident&#8217;s expected address and telephone number after
   discharge; and
   
   
   
   (g)	The identity of the person(s) responsible for the resident after
   discharge, where applicable.

   6275.2. For residents in emergency care facilities and runaway shelters,
   discharge planning shall begin commensurately with the resident&#8217;s
   admission.

   6275.3. The discharge plan shall be developed by a planning team consisting of
   the administrator, the contracting entity, and staff who will have direct
   responsibility for implementing the discharge plan. Where feasible, the
   resident, the resident&#8217;s parent(s) or guardian(s), and the
   resident&#8217;s guardian ad litem shall be involved in the development of the
   discharge plan.

   6275.4. In the case of a planned discharge, the facility shall work with the
   resident in advance of the date of discharge to assure a smooth transition.

   6275.5. The facility may not discharge a resident without the agreement of the
   contracting entity and, where applicable, the CSSD.

   6275.6. Upon the resident&#8217;s discharge from the facility, the facility
   shall turn over the original or a copy of the resident&#8217;s case record to
   the contracting entity.
   
   
   
   	SOURCE: Final Rulemaking published at 48 DCR 8675, 8739 (September 21, 2001).
