                                  DC REGULATIONS

CASE RECORD (§ 29-6318)

6318.1. An independent living program shall maintain a separate written case
   record for each resident.

   6318.2. The independent living program shall make timely entries in the case
   record that are legible, dated, and signed by the staff member or other person
   making the entry.

   6318.3. A resident&#8217;s case record shall include:
   
   
   
   (a)	Name, date and place of birth, social security number, date of admission,
   and citizenship;
   
   
   
   (b)	A physical description, including but not limited to sex, race, height,
   weight, hair color, eye color, and identifying marks;
   
   
   
   (c)	A current photograph;
   
   
   
   (d)	The primary language or means of communication spoken and understood by
   the resident and the primary language used by the resident&#8217;s family, if
   other than English;
   
   
   
   (e)	Religious affiliation, if any;
   
   
   
   (f)	The name, address, and telephone number of the resident&#8217;s parent(s)
   or other responsible family member(s), guardian, attorney, and referring
   agency or case worker, as applicable;
   
   
   
   (g)	Medical, mental health, and immunization records, including but not
   limited to identified emergency medical needs, allergies, basic needs, and
   non-emergency medical conditions and physical infirmities, including all
   visible signs of illness or injury;
   
   
   
   (h)	Dental, vision, and hearing records;
   
   
   
   (i)	The social history;
   
   
   
   (j)	The contracting entity&#8217;s case plan, if any;
   
   
   
   (k)	The initial ITILP as required by § 6341 and any changes or updates
   thereto;
   
   
   
   (l)	The ITILP as required by § 6341 and any changes or updates thereto;
   
   
   
   (m)	The discharge plan, as required by § 6348;
   
   
   
   (n)	Behavior management and discipline records relating to the resident, where
   applicable, as required by § 6347;
   
   
   
   (o)	All correspondence relevant to the resident;
   
   
   
   (p)	Reports of unusual incidents as required by § 6319;
   
   
   
   (q)	Reports related to abuse, neglect, or other risks to the resident&#8217;s
   health and safety made pursuant to § 6304;
   
   
   
   (r)	Any consents to treatment as required by § 6342.7;
   
   
   
   (s)	Any restrictions on visitation, mail, or telephone contacts;
   
   
   
   (t)	Court orders, if applicable;
   
   
   
   (u)	Admission and placement information as required by § 6339;
   
   
   
   (v)	Signed notification of rights as required by § 6340.3(f);
   
   
   
   (w)	Signed notification of grievance procedures, as required by § 6340.3(g);
   
   
   
   (x)	Education records;
   
   
   
   (y)	Any evaluation or progress reports prepared for the resident;
   
   
   
   (z)	The resident&#8217;s grievance record; and
   
   
   
   (aa)	Documentation of counseling and treatment sessions.

   6318.4. An independent living program shall maintain all information in a
   resident&#8217;s case record for so long as the resident is admitted to the
   independent living program. Upon the resident&#8217;s discharge from the
   independent living program, the independent living program shall turn over the
   original case record to the contracting entity.

   6318.5. An independent living program shall maintain a resident&#8217;s case
   record in a locked and secure location when unattended.

   6318.6. An independent living program, CFSA, the contracting entity, and the
   guardian ad litem shall have access to a resident&#8217;s case record. No
   other person may have access to the resident&#8217;s case record unless
   authorized in writing by the CFSA.

   6318.7. An independent living program shall maintain in the case record easily
   accessible emergency information for each resident, including:
   
   
   
   (a)	The name, address, telephone number, and relationship of a designated
   person to be contacted in case of an emergency;
   
   
   
   (b)	If the resident is less than eighteen (18) years of age, a person able to
   give consent for emergency medical treatment, if applicable;
   
   
   
   (c)	The resident&#8217;s physician or source of health care; and
   
   
   
   (d)	Health insurance information.
   
   
   
   	SOURCE: Final Rulemaking published at 49 DCR 1591 (February 22, 2002).
