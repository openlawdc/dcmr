                                  DC REGULATIONS

DEFINITIONS (ยง 29-982)

Accrual Method of Accounting &#8211; a method of accounting pursuant to which
revenue is recorded in the period earned, regardless of when collected and
expenses are recorded in the period incurred, regarding of when paid. See: Final
Rulemaking published at 45 DCR 2333, 2350 (April 17, 1998).



Actual Time Method &#8211; the number of months the asset is owned both the year
of acquisition and the year of disposal. See: Final Rulemaking published at 45
DCR 2333, 2350 (April 17, 1998).



Acuity &#8211; the level of care required for an individual in an ICF/MR which
correlates to an acuity level. Customers with a high acuity level require more
care, those with lower acity levels require less care. See: Final Rulemaking
published at 45 DCR 2333, 2350 (April 17, 1998).



Allowable costs &#8211; those actual costs after appropriate adjustments,
incurred by an ICF/MR facility which are reimbursable under the Medicaid
program. See: Final Rulemaking published at 45 DCR 2333, 2350 (April 17, 1998).



Average Per Diem Cost &#8211; See: Final Rulemaking published at 45 DCR 2333,

Active treatment services &#8211; A program of specialized and generic training,
treatment, health services and related services designed toward the acquisition
of the behaviors necessary for the client to function with as much self
determination and independence as possible, and the prevention or deceleration
of regression or loss of current optimal functional status. This definition
shall be consistent with the federal standard.
