SELECTION CRITERIA (ยง 29-3103)

3103.1. To participate in the Nurses Training Corps, a person shall meet all
   of the following selection criteria:
   
   
   
   (a)	Be a bona fide resident of the District of Columbia for two (2) years
   immediately prior to application to the Corps;
   
   
   
   (b)	Have a stated interest in nursing and public service in the field of
   nursing within the District;
   
   
   
   (c)	Have proof of admission or enrollment as a full-time student in a District
   of Columbia institution of higher education that has a Department, School or
   College of Nursing or a course of study in nursing and is currently accredited
   by a nationally recognized accrediting agency or association;
   
   
   
   (d)	Pursue a course of study leading to a certificate or degree in the field
   of nursing that is not a course of graduate study; and
   
   
   
   (e)	Have a financial need as computed under the college scholarship needs
   analysis or needs analysis approved by the Director.

   3103.2. In making a final selection among competing applicants that meet the
   above criteria, the Director shall consider the following factors:
   
   
   
   (a)	Student graduates with a 2.5 or better Grade Point Average or General
   Equivalency Diploma equivalent;
   
   
   
   (b)	Number of available slots at the educational institution of choice;
   
   
   
   (c)	Level of financial assistance needed; and
   
   
   
   (d)	Date of completed application.
   
   
   
   	SOURCE: Final Rulemaking published at 37 DCR 8290, 8291 (December 21, 1990).
