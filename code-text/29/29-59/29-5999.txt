                                  DC REGULATIONS

DEFINITIONS (ยง 29-5999)

5999.1. The following terms and phrases shall have the meanings described:
   
   
   
   &#8220;Appellant&#8221; &#8211; An applicant, recipient, person identified in
   a Child Protection Register report, or a licensee of a foster, youth
   residential facility, or independent living program who is appealing a CFSA
   decision, or a legal representative.
   
   
   
   &#8220;Applicant&#8221; &#8211; A person who applies for a subsidy or to
   become a licensed foster parent, to operate a youth residential facility or to
   operate an independent living program.
   
   
   
   &#8220;Business day&#8221; &#8211; Monday through Friday excluding legal
   holidays.
   
   
   
   &#8220;CFSA&#8221; &#8211; The District of Columbia Child and Family Services
   Agency.
   
   
   
   &#8220;Child Protection Register&#8221; &#8211; The confidential index of all
   reports of suspected child abuse or neglect established pursuant to The
   Prevention of Child Abuse and Neglect Act of 1977, effective September 23,
   1977, D.C. Law 2-22, title II, ยง 201, or its equivalent in another
   jurisdiction.
   
   
   
   &#8220;Court&#8221; &#8211; The District of Columbia Superior Court.
   
   
   
   &#8220;Director&#8221; &#8211; The Director of the D.C. Child and Family
   Services Agency or his or her designee, or the administrative head of any
   successor agency.
   
   
   
   &#8220;Emergency&#8221; &#8211; A situation that constitutes an immediate or
   serious and continuing danger to the health, safety or welfare of residents or
   staff.
   
   
   
   &#8220;Emergency suspension or conversion&#8221; &#8211; The suspension or
   conversion of a license to operate a foster home, youth residential facility
   or independent living program on an emergency basis without prior notice or
   hearing.
   
   
   
   &#8220;Hearing Examiner&#8221; &#8211; The individual appointed by the
   Director to conduct fair hearings and make final decisions under the
   regulations.
   
   
   
   &#8220;Independent living program&#8221; &#8211; A program licensed pursuant
   to 29 DCMR Chapter 63.
   
   
   
   &#8220;Licensing Record&#8221; &#8211; the files and records maintained by
   CFSA, other than information required by law to be kept confidential, that
   pertains to the decision regarding licensing.
   
   
   
   &#8220;Licensed foster parent&#8221; &#8211; A person who is licensed as a
   foster parent in accordance with 29 DCMR Chapter 60.
   
   
   
   &#8220;Party&#8221; &#8211; The appellant and CFSA through the Program
   Administrator or designee.
   
   
   
   &#8220;Recipient&#8221; &#8211; A person who applies for and receives a
   subsidy from CFSA.
   
   
   
   &#8220;Representative&#8221; &#8211; Any person, including a non attorney,
   authorized by an appellant in writing to represent the appellant in a fair
   hearing.
   
   
   
   &#8220;Service&#8221; &#8211; The provision of a subsidy or any other child
   welfare service for which a fair hearing is permitted under this Chapter.
   
   
   
   &#8220;Subsidy&#8221; &#8211; A monthly payment made to certain adoptive or
   permanent guardianship families to financially support care of a child who was
   placed in the permanent placement.
   
   
   
   &#8220;Youth residential facility&#8221; &#8211; A facility licensed pursuant
   to 29 DCMR Chapter 62.
