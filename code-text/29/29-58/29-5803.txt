                                  DC REGULATIONS

CONFIDENTIALITY (ยง 29-5803)

5803.1. For the purposes of this chapter, the following definitions shall
   apply:
   
   
   
   (a)	&#8220;Information concerning applicants and recipients&#8221; means
   documents and records, and all information contained therein, that are:
   
   
   
   (1)	Related to an individual&#8217;s application for or receipt of TANF or
   POWER benefits; and
   
   
   
   (2)	Maintained by the District government or an entity contracting with the
   District government;
   
   
   
   (b)	&#8220;Services for applicants and recipients&#8221; means assistance
   directly provided by the District government or an entity contracting with the
   District government; and
   
   
   
   (c)	Notwithstanding paragraph (a) of this subsection, aggregate information
   that is compiled for statistical purposes and may not be used to identify an
   individual or a family shall not be considered &#8220;information concerning
   applicants and recipients&#8221; that is subject to confidentiality
   restrictions.

   5803.2. By signing an application or recertification form for TANF or POWER
   benefits, an applicant or recipient shall consent to the disclosure, to the
   extent permitted under District law, of all information concerning the
   applicant or recipient that is required by DHS to determine or redetermine
   eligibility or to provide services to the applicant or recipient.

   5803.3. If a TANF or POWER applicant or recipient, the applicant&#8217;s or
   recipient&#8217;s currently authorized representative, or a person acting on
   the applicant&#8217;s or recipient&#8217;s behalf makes a written request to
   review material and information contained in the applicant&#8217;s or
   recipient&#8217;s case file, the material and information contained in the
   case file shall be made available for inspection during normal business hours,
   upon reasonable notice.

   5803.4. DHS may withhold confidential information, such as the names of
   individuals who have disclosed information about the household without the
   household&#8217;s knowledge, or the nature or status of pending criminal
   prosecutions.

   5803.5. A TANF or POWER applicant or recipient may execute a written waiver of
   his or her privilege of confidentiality.

   5803.6. No employee of the District government or an entity contracting with
   the District government may release information concerning the applicant or
   recipient (including name, address, or other identifying information), unless
   a TANF or POWER applicant signs a written waiver; except as expressly
   permitted in Section 904 of the District of Columbia Public Assistance Act of
   1982, as amended.

   5803.7. A person who lawfully receives confidential information concerning a
   TANF or POWER applicant or recipient shall adequately protect the information
   against disclosure to any persons or for purposes not authorized by law.

   5803.8. No person shall disclose, inspect, or use documents or records in
   violation of this section.
   
   
   
   	SOURCE: Final Rulemaking published at 48 DCR 10251 (November 9, 2001).
