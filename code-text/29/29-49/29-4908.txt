                                  DC REGULATIONS

ADJUSTMENT(S) TO THE PAYMENT RATE (ยง 29-4908)

4908.1. After completion of an audit of CFSA&#8217;s reported costs, MAA shall
   provide CFSA with a written notice of its determination of any adjustment to
   the payment rate. The notice shall include the following:
   
   
   
   (a)	A description of the rate adjustment, including the original payment rate
   and the revised payment rate;
   
   
   
   (b)	The effective date of the change in the payment rate;
   
   
   
   (c)	The amount of money due to or from MAA attributable to the change in the
   payment rate;
   
   
   
   (d)	A summary of all audit adjustments made to CFSA&#8217;s reported costs,
   including an explanation, by appropriate reference to law, rules, or the
   program manual, of the reason in support of the adjustment; and
   
   
   
   (e)	A statement informing CFSA of the right to request an administrative
   review within sixty (60) days of the date of the determination.

   4908.2. CFSA shall have sixty days from the date of the determination issued
   pursuant to subsection 4908.1 to submit a written request for administrative
   review if it disagrees with any audit adjustment or payment rate calculation.
   The request for administrative review shall be submitted to the Financial
   Manager, Audit and Finance Office, Medical Assistance Administration,
   Department of Health.

   4908.3. The written request for administrative review shall include a specific
   description of the audit adjustment or payment rate calculation to be
   reviewed, the reason for the review, the relief requested, and any
   documentation in support of the relief requested.

   4908.4. MAA shall mail a written determination relative to the administrative
   review not later than one hundred and twenty (120) days from the date of the
   written request for administrative review under 4908.1.

   4908.5. Within forty five (45) days of receipt of the MAA&#8217;s written
   determination, CFSA may appeal the written determination by filing a written
   notice of appeal with the Board of Appeals and Review.

   4908.6. Filing an appeal shall not stay any action to recover any overpayment.
   
   
   
   	SOURCE: Emergency Rulemaking published at 48 DCR 8872 (September 21, 2001)
   [EXPIRED]; as amended by Final Rulemaking published at 49 DCR 247 (January 11,
