IMPORTATION OF ALCOHOLIC BEVERAGES FOR PRIVATE USE AND CONSUMPTION (ยง 23-1302)

1302.1. Any person who becomes a resident of, or establishes a domicile in,
   the District of Columbia, may bring into the District of Columbia his or her
   own personal stock of alcoholic beverages, so long as the beverages are for
   his or her private use and consumption and so long as the importation of the
   beverages is in compliance with applicable laws and regulations.

   1302.2. The provisions of ยง 1302.1 shall apply to embassies and diplomatic
   representatives of foreign countries.

   1302.3. Prior to importing under this section any alcoholic beverages into the
   District of Columbia from outside of the United States, the individual seeking
   to do so shall submit a written request for an importation permit to the Board
   which contains the following information:
   
   
   
   (a)	The type, brand, and quantity of each beverage to be imported;
   
   
   
   (b)	The individual&#8217;s out-of-state or foreign address and telephone
   number;
   
   
   
   (c)	The individual&#8217;s permanent address and telephone number in the
   District of Columbia; and
   
   
   
   (d)	A statement that the beverages are for personal use and consumption and
   will not be sold.

   1302.4. The fee for an Importation Permit for alcoholic beverages imported
   pursuant to ยง 1302 for private use and consumption shall be fifty dollars
   ($50), paid by credit card, certified check, money order, business check,
   attorney&#8217;s check, or personal check payable to ABRA.
   
   
   
   SOURCE: Final Rulemaking published at 51 DCR 4309 (April 30, 2004); as amended
   by Final Rulemaking published at 55 DCR 12991 (December 26, 2008).
