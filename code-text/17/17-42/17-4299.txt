                                  DC REGULATIONS

DEFINITIONS (§ 17-4299)

4299.1. As used in this chapter, the following terms have the meaning
   ascribed:
   
   
   
   Act &#8211; The District of Columbia Health Occupation Revision Act of 1985
   (&#8220;Act&#8221;), effective March 25, 1986 (D.C. Law 6-99; D.C. Official
   Code § 3-1201.01 et seq.)
   
   
   
   ADEX- American Board of Dental Examiners.
   
   
   
   Applicant &#8211; a person applying for a license to practice dentistry under
   this chapter.
   
   
   
   Auxiliary &#8211; means a person who may perform dental supportive procedures
   authorized by District of Columbia law or regulations under the specified
   supervision of a licensed dentist.
   
   
   
   Bleaching &#8211; external tooth whitening procedures.
   
   
   
   Bloodborne pathogen- means pathogenic microorganisms that are present in human
   blood and can cause disease in humans. These pathogens include, but are not
   limited to, hepatitis B virus (HBV), hepatitis C virus (HCV) and human
   immunodeficiency virus (HIV).
   
   
   
   Board &#8211; the Board of Dentistry, established by § 201 of the Act, D.C.
   Code § 2-3302.1 (1987 Supp.).
   
   
   
   Dental hygienist &#8211; a person licensed to practice dental hygiene under
   the Act.
   
   
   
   Dentist &#8211; a person licensed to practice dentistry under the Act.
   
   
   
   NERB &#8211; the North East Regional Board of Dental Examiners, Inc.
   
   
   
   NERB Examination—the examination in dentistry offered by the North East
   Regional Board of Dental Examiners, Inc.; or its successor.
   
   
   
   Passed a Regional Board &#8211; means that an applicant has earned a score of
   seventy-five (75%) in each discipline, clinical skill, procedure, or knowledge
   that is tested on the NERB examination using the internal weighting and
   scoring methods the NERB uses to score the NERB&#8217;s examination of
   dentistry or dental hygiene as applicable.
   
   
   
   Regional Board – the examination in dentistry offered by any of the
   following dental examination organizations:
   
   
   
   (a)	The Central Regional Dental Testing Service;
   
   
   
   (b)	The North East Regional Board of Dental Examiners, Inc.;
   
   
   
   (c)	The Southern Regional Testing Agency, Inc.; and
   
   
   
   (d)	The Western Regional Examining Board, Inc.
   
   
   
   Universal precautions &#8211; means blood and body fluid precautions as
   defined by the Center for Disease Control.

   4299.2. The definition in §4099 of Chapter 40 of this title are incorporated
   by reference into and are applicable to this chapter.
