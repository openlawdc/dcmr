                                  DC REGULATIONS

DEFINITIONS (§ 17-5999)

5999.1. For purposes of this chapter, the following terms have the meanings
   ascribed:
   
   
   
   Act &#8211; Health Occupation Revision Act of 1985 (“Act”), effective
   March 25, 1986 (D.C. Law 6-99; D.C. Official Code § 3-1201.01 et seq.).
   
   
   
   Applicant &#8211; a person applying for a license to practice as a
   nurse-practitioner under this chapter.
   
   
   
   Board &#8211; the Board of Nursing, established by § 204 of the Act, D.C.
   Official Code § 3-1202.04.
   
   
   
   Certificate &#8211; a certificate to practice as a nurse practitioner under
   this chapter.
   
   
   
   Certified nurse practitioner &#8211; a registered nurse trained in an
   educational program and certified by a recognized national certification
   organization to provide healthcare services who, when functioning within the
   authorized scope of practice, is qualified to assume primary responsibility
   for the care of patients.
   
   
   
   Clinical practice &#8211; the routine application of the principles of a
   nurse-practitioner to the diagnosis and treatment of disease and the
   maintenance of health.
   
   
   
   Graduate &#8211; an individual who has graduated from a post-basic nursing
   education program for preparation to practice as a nurse practitioner.
   
   
   
   Supervisor &#8211; a registered nurse-practitioner certified under the Act who
   is responsible for the supervision of a student, graduate, or applicant.

   5999.2. The definitions in § 4099 of chapter 40 of this title are
   incorporated by reference into and are applicable to this chapter.
