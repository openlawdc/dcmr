                                  DC REGULATIONS

SUPERVISING SURGEON (ยง 17-8014)

8014.1. To be authorized to supervise a surgical assistant, a physician must
   be currently licensed as a physician in the District. The license must be
   unrestricted and active.

   8014.2. A supervising surgeon shall perform the critical portions of a
   surgical procedure. Supervision shall be continuous, and shall require that
   the delegating physician be immediately available in the surgical suite for
   delegated acts that the surgical assistant performs and to respond to any
   emergency until the patient is released from the surgical suite and care has
   been transferred to another physician, or until the surgical assistant has
   completed his or her tasks and has been excused by the supervising surgeon.
   Telecommunication is insufficient for supervision purposes or as a means for
   directing delegated acts.

   8014.3. It is the responsibility of each team of physician(s) and surgical
   assistant(s) to ensure that:
   
   
   
   (a)	The surgical assistant&#8217;s scope of practice is clearly defined;
   
   
   
   (b)	Delegation of medical tasks is appropriate to the surgical
   assistant&#8217;s level of competence;
   
   
   
   (c)	The relationship between the members of the team is defined;
   
   
   
   (d)	That the relationship of, and access to, the supervising surgeon is
   defined; and
   
   
   
   (e)	A process for evaluation of the surgical assistant&#8217;s performance is
   established.
   
   
   
   	SOURCE: Final Rulemaking published at 54 DCR 9549 (October 5, 2007).
