                                  DC REGULATIONS

DUTIES OF COLLABORATING PHYSICIAN TO ACUPUNCTURIST (ยง 17-4714)

4714.1. A collaborating physician shall give written authorization to the
   acupuncturist to initiate treatment. The authorization shall contain the
   following:
   
   
   
   (a)	A summary of the findings of the physician&#8217;s examination of the
   patient; and
   
   
   
   (b)	Restrictions, if any, to be placed on the course of acupuncture treatment.

   4714.2. A collaborating physician shall be available to the acupuncturist on a
   daily basis for consultation by a communication device regarding the
   patient&#8217;s condition.

   4714.3. A collaborating physician shall designate a physician to be available
   for consultation in the temporary absence of the collaborating physician and
   shall notify the acupuncturist of the following:
   
   
   
   (a)	The name;
   
   
   
   (b)	The telephone number; and
   
   
   
   (c)	The office address of the back-up physician.

   4714.4. For the purposes of this subsection, a collaborating physician shall
   be considered absent if the collaborating physician:
   
   
   
   (a)	Travels beyond a thirty (30) mile radius of the District; or
   
   
   
   (b)	Is unable to practice medicine because of illness, injury, attention to
   personal needs, or other conflict with the requirements of the practice.

   4714.5. A collaborating physician shall provide written notice to the
   acupuncturist of the collaborating physician&#8217;s intent to terminate the
   collaboration prior to terminating the collaboration.
   
   
   
   	SOURCE: Final Rulemaking published at 36 DCR 7159, 7165 (October 13, 1989).
