6499.1. As used in this chapter, the following terms have the meanings

   	Applicant &#8211; a person applying for a license to practice optometry under
   	this chapter.
   
   
   
   Board &#8211; the Board of Optometry, established by ยง 207 of the Act, D.C.

   Contact Lens Fitting &#8211; the process that begins after the initial eye
   examination and ends when a successful fit has been achieved as determined by
   the examining optometrist or ophthalmologist. In the case of a renewal
   prescription, the fitting ends when the prescriber determines that no change

   medically necessary follow-up examinations.
   
   
   
   	Continuing competency:

   			achieving appropriate outcomes and maintaining or improving the
   				patients quality of life;

   management of the overall health needs and to encourage the well-being of
   patients;
   
   
   
   
   
   
   
   The ability to apply management skills to optimize the care of  patients and
   make efficient use of health resources; and
   
   
   
   
   
   
   
   The ability to educate patients with the goal of encouraging appropriate,
   effective, and comprehensive eye and vision care.
   
   
   
   
   
   Ophthalmologist &#8211; a physician specializing in ophthalmology.
   
   
   
   Optometrist &#8211; a person licensed to practice optometry under the Act.
   
   
   
   Physician &#8211; a person licensed to practice medicine under the Act.
   
   
   
   Private label contact lenses &#8211; contact lenses that are sold under the
   label of a seller where the contact lenses are identical to lenses made by the
   same manufacturer but sold under the labels of other sellers.
