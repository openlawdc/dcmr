                                  DC REGULATIONS

DEFINITIONS (§ 17-5099)

5099.1. As used in this chapter, the following terms have the meaning
   ascribed:
   
   
   
   Applicant &#8211; a person making application to be registered as a
   naturopath.
   
   
   
   &#8220;Naturally occurring substances&#8221; means food natural vitamins and
   minerals, herbs and herbal preparations, roots, barks, homeopathic
   preparations, plant and natural medicines of animal, mineral or botanical
   origin which do not require a prescription unless from a homeopathic pharmacy.
   
   
   
   &#8220;Counseling individuals and treatment through the use of naturally
   occurring substances&#8221; includes, but is not limited to, giving advice,
   ordering or prescribing naturally occurring substances, and the use of
   physical modalities, and other treatments, commonly provided by naturopaths
   and not otherwise prohibited under the Act.
   
   
   
   &#8220;Surgical procedures&#8221; refer to the medically accepted definition
   of surgery, but shall not include care incident to superficial lacerations and
   abrasions, and the removal of foreign bodies located in superficial
   structures, no to include the eye, and the use of antiseptics and topical
   local anesthetics in connection therewith.
   
   
   
   “Drug, substance, or device&#8221; means a prescription or drug, substance
   or a device but shall not include “naturally occurring substances&#8221;.

   5099.2. The definitions in section 4099 of Chapter 40 of this are incorporated
   by reference into and are applicable to this chapter.
   
   
   
   	SOURCE: Final Rulemaking published at 39 DCR 6129 (August 14, 1992).
