5800.1. A certified nurse-midwife is a registered nurse prepared in a formal
   educational program to assume an expanded role in providing health care in the
   area of nurse-midwifery services. Certified nurse-midwives, when functioning
   within the authorized scope of practice, are qualified to assume primary
   responsibility for the care of their patients. This practice incorporates the
   use of independent judgment as well as collaborative interaction with
   physicians or osteopaths.

   5800.2. Chapters 40 (Health Occupations: General Rules), 41 (Health
   Occupations: Administrative Procedures), and 54 (Registered Nursing) of this
   title shall supplement this chapter.
