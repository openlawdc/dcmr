                                  DC REGULATIONS

FACIAL PROCEDURES, DEVICES, AND EQUIPMENT (§ 17-3729)

3729.1. Cosmetic exfoliating substances may include alpha hydroxy acids,
   glycolic and lactic acids, beta hydroxy acids, salicylic acid, Jessner’s
   Solutions, resorcinol, and other substances intended to exfoliate the stratum
   corneum.

   3729.2. Licensees are prohibited from performing procedures using cosmetic
   exfoliating substances or devices that affect more than the top layer of the
   epidermis or outer-most layer of dead cells.  A cosmetic exfoliation substance
   or device is not intended to remove viable (living) skin below the stratum
   corneum. Use of such substances and devices are deemed beyond the scope of
   practice of persons and establishments licensed by the Board.

   3729.3. Licensees shall be permitted to use devices registered or approved by
   the U.S. Food and Drug Administration that are intended to be used for
   cosmetic skin care purposes, including, but not limited to, beautifying and
   improving the appearance of the skin.
