                                  DC REGULATIONS

DEFINITIONS (§ 17-7099)

7099.1. As used in this chapter, the following terms have the meanings
   ascribed:
   
   
   
   Applicant &#8211; a person applying under this chapter for a license as a
   social work associate, graduate social worker, independent social worker, or
   independent clinical social worker, as the context requires.
   
   
   
   Board &#8211; the Board of Social Work, established by §212 of the Act, D.C.
   Code §2-3302.12 (1987 Supp.).
   
   
   
   Good cause &#8211; serious illness of the applicant; the death or serious
   illness of a member of the applicant&#8217;s immediate family; or other cause
   sufficient to the Board.
   
   
   
   Immediate Supervision – one-to-one supervision in which the supervisor is
   physically present with the supervisee and either discussing or observing the
   supervisee’s practice; and shall not include telephone communication, video
   conferencing, or internet communication.
   
   
   
   Mandatory D.C. Social Work Laws and Regulations Review Course – the one (1)
   hour, on-line course that the D.C. Board of Social Work offers consisting of a
   comprehensive review of the laws and regulations governing the practice of
   social work in the District of Columbia.
   
   
   
   National Examination &#8211; the social work licensing examination sponsored
   by the Association of Social Work Boards.
   
   
   
   Social Worker &#8211; a person licensed under the Act to practice social work.
   
   
   
   Supervisee – an applicant or social worker authorized to engage in
   supervised practice under § 7012.
   
   
   
   Supervision &#8211; A formalized professional relationship between a
   supervisor and supervisee in which the supervisor directs, monitors, and
   evaluates the supervisee&#8217;s social work practice while promoting
   development of the supervisee&#8217;s knowledge, skills, and abilities to
   provide social work services in an ethical and competent manner.
   
   
   
   Supervisor &#8211; a social worker licensed under the Act, or a qualified
   supervisor under § 7013, as the context requires, who is responsible for the
   supervision of a supervisee.

   7099.2. The definitions in §4099 of chapter 40 of this title are incorporated
   by reference into and apply to this chapter.
