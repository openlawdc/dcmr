                                  DC REGULATIONS

EVALUATION STANDARDS (ยง 28-2031)

2031.1. A judge declaring candidacy for reappointment shall be evaluated by
   the Commission through a review of the judge&#8217;s performance and conduct
   during the judge&#8217;s present term of office.

   2031.2. The evaluation categories shall include the following:
   
   
   
   (a)	Well Qualified &#8211; The candidate&#8217;s work product, legal
   scholarship, dedication, efficiency, and demeanor are exceptional, and the
   candidate&#8217;s performance consistently reflects credit on the judicial
   system.
   
   
   
   (b)	Qualified &#8211; The candidate satisfactorily performs the judicial
   function or, if there are negative traits, they are overcome by strong
   positive attributes.
   
   
   
   (c)	Unqualified &#8211; The candidate is unfit for further judicial service.
   
   
   
   SOURCE: Final Rulemaking published at 24 DCR 9391, 9395 (May 5, 1978); as
   amended by Final Rulemaking published at 34 DCR 2190, 2192 (April 3, 1987);
   Final Rulemaking published at 37 DCR 6032, 6043 (September 14, 1990); and
   Final Rulemaking published at 39 DCR 9333, 9344 (December 11, 1992); as
   amended by Final Rulemaking published at 53 DCR 108 (January 6, 2006); as
   amended by Final Rulemaking published at 54 DCR 12322 (December 21, 2007).
