                                  DC REGULATIONS

PAYMENT PLANS IN CIVIL INFRACTIONS ACT CASES (ยง 1-2806)

2806.1. If an Administrative Law Judge has imposed monetary sanctions under
   the Civil Infractions Act, a Respondent may request to pay the monetary
   sanctions in installments.  An Administrative Law Judge may permit installment
   payments for no more than six (6) months beyond the date of the final order
   and may charge a fee of one percent (1%) per month of the outstanding amount.

   2806.2. In requesting a payment plan under this Section, a Respondent shall
   state, in writing, the reasons for seeking a payment plan, the length of time
   requested, and why Respondent cannot afford to pay the entire monetary
   sanction in a lump sum.

   2806.3. A Respondent must file with OAH and serve on the Government a request
   for a payment plan within thirty (30) calendar days of the service of the
   final order.

   2806.4. The Government may file with OAH a response to a request for a payment
   plan within five (5) calendar days of the service of the request.
   
   
   
   	SOURCE: Final Rulemaking published at 51 DCR 6399 (June 25, 2004); as amended
   by Notice of Final Rulemaking published at 57 DCR 12541, 12546 (December 31,
   2010).
